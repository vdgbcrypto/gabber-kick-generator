# Gabber Kick Generator — 20 Techno Presets

## Techno Kick Design Principles

A techno kick is fundamentally a sine oscillator whose pitch is swept downward by a fast exponential envelope: it launches from a "punch" frequency (~150–220 Hz) and drops to a deep sub (45–55 Hz) within a few milliseconds, producing the body "thud" without obvious pitch wobble. The amplitude envelope is kept short-to-medium (120–400 ms) for tightness, and the click transient is subtle and low-passed so the kick sits under hats/bass rather than fighting them — unlike gabber, techno favors cleanliness and depth over aggression. Tuning the sub to the track's musical root (A1=55, G1=49, F#1=46.25, F1=43.7, E1=41.2) keeps the kick harmonically locked to the bassline.
